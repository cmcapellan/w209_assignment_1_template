This chart shows my active calories for each week as measured by my Apple Watch starting at the beginning of 2019 (the first week started on 12/30/2018) through the end of August 2020.

Apple defines "Active Calories" as calories burned while actively moving around such as when walking or exercising. This metric does not include calories from a person's Basal Metabolic Rate (which is based on age, gender, height and weight).

The main insight from this data for me is the negative impact on my activity caused by the start of the pandemic in March 2020. I had big dropoff which I was able to rebound from in April, but then I've still trailed off in the summer of 2020.

Problem Abstraction
What We have a table of data, each item is a week which has two quantitative attributes, the start date and active calories, the data is from the student.
Why The visual task is to summarize the data into weekly periods and show trends in active calories.
How The mark is a line chart, here it using position on a common scale. The area below the line is colored in for visual effect, but the information is presented the same as a standard line chart.
Insight Active calories dropped after the start of the pandemic and are now lower than in the start of the year.
Insight I had actually been on a good upswing since the end of summer 2019.
